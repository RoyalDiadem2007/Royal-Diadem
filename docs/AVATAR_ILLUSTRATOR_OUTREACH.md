**Subject:** Commission — layered avatar illustration for a youth nonprofit app

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your name], [role] at **Royal Diadem**, a Houston nonprofit that has walked alongside **young
Black women ages 11–19** since 2007, building confidence and sisterhood. We're putting the finishing
touch on an app for the girls we serve — and one of its warmest features lets each girl **build her
own avatar** for her "Queen Card." We'd love to commission you to create that avatar art. [Your work
on **[specific piece / their style]** is exactly the warmth and authenticity we're after.]

**Here's what matters most.** The girls are Black, and how they see *themselves* in the app is the
whole point. So the art has to be real:

- **Hair rendered right** — afros, coils, locs, box braids, cornrows, puffs and ponytails, with
  genuine texture, worn correctly (pulled back off the face, not draped over it).
- **The full range of Black features, with dignity** — varied nose widths, a full-lips option, rich
  deep skin tones — drawn beautifully and warmly, never as caricature.
- **Cute and aspirational.** We treat these girls as royalty; the crown is part of the brand.

This is not a generic avatar set. Authenticity is the assignment.

**We've done the technical homework so you can focus on the art.** Attached is a complete,
build-ready spec. It defines everything that usually causes back-and-forth: the canvas and alignment
points, the layer order, a simple recolour approach (**you draw each hairstyle once** and the app
tints it to every skin tone / hair colour), exact colour palettes, per-option art direction, file
naming, and acceptance criteria. The app side is already built — your artwork drops straight in, no
development work on your end.

**At a glance, the set is around three dozen layered SVGs:** 6 skin tones (tinted, not redrawn),
5 face shapes, 5 eye / 4 nose / 5 mouth options, 7 hairstyles (each in 5 hair colours, tinted),
5 crowns, plus brows and blush. Full detail is in the spec.

For authentic textured and protective styles, we point to **Code My Crown** (Dove × Open Source Afro
Hair Library) — a free reference created by Black artists — in the spec.

**To put together a quote, we'd love to know:**

1. A few samples of relevant work — especially **Black character / textured-hair illustration**.
2. Your availability and a realistic timeline.
3. Your fee.
4. That you can **assign full commercial rights** to Royal Diadem (a product serving minors) —
   perpetual, worldwide, including the right to modify and recolour.

**One suggestion to save us both time:** start with a **single fully composed sample avatar** so we
can confirm alignment and style before you produce the full set.

rate]**, and we're hoping to have the art by **[date]**.]

Thank you for considering this — it means a lot for the girls who'll see themselves in it.
